Case Study

The Human Algorithm: Why the Future of Learning at UNC Charlotte Must Start with the Soul

CHARLOTTE, NC — On the third floor of the J. Murrey Atkins Library, the rhythmic, mechanical clicking of keyboards creates a frantic symphony. Among the hundreds of students hunched over glowing screens is Addison, a sophomore at UNC Charlotte.

Addison is a first-generation college student facing the "Repetitive Rigor" trap—a cycle of automated quizzes and hollow discussion posts that feel like a barrier to his creative soul and ADHD-driven need for novelty.

"I'm learning more in those twenty minutes of 'crafting' than in two hours of typing 'I agree' into a forum."

The Challenge

How do we shift from technology as a medium for submission to technology as a tool for creation? How can we empower Addison to manifest his understanding through dramatic monologues, digital art, or scores, rather than just filling a word count?

Workshop Objective

Brainstorm concrete ways Addison can use AI (GEMs, NotebookLM) to amplify his human skills. How can AI act as a collaborative partner in his creative process?
