Human-Centered AI (HCAI)

Program Launch Status & Curricular Framework

The Office of Interdisciplinary Studies is pleased to announce that both the B.A. Concentration in Human-Centered AI and the Minor in Artificial Intelligence Fundamentals have successfully cleared the Curriculog process and are officially active within Banner. Registration will go live for the student body next week, initiating our official Fall 2026 launch.

Fall 2026 Inaugural Course Offerings

To support immediate student demand, we are offering two sections of each core course:

HCAI 1100 · AI Fundamentals and Literacy for All (3 credits)

Develops critical engagement with intelligent systems, evaluating computational frameworks from early thinking machines through to modern generative models.

HCAI 2100 · Computing, Labor, and Power in History and Society (3 credits)

An examination of labor dynamics, structural biases, and institutional frameworks through a humanistic and social-science lens.

HCAI 2101 · AI Application and Impact Across Disciplines (3 credits)

Equips students with the framework to utilize artificial intelligence safely and responsibly while evaluating cross-disciplinary consequences.

Advanced & Interdisciplinary Pathways

Beyond the core sequences, students have immediate access to specialized, cross-listed courses. These include HCAI 3111 (Social Sciences Applied) and HCAI 3112 (Humanities Applied), designed in coordination with departments across The College of Humanities & Earth and Social Sciences to anchor AI Literacy with the core human-centered skills of an AI ready graduate.

Degree Architecture

Total Degree Requirement: 120 Credit Hours

  • Concentration Core (21 hrs) Includes foundational sequences, interdisciplinary applications, research methods (IDST 3100), and the final Capstone (HCAI 4600).
  • Disciplinary Depth (18 hrs) Designed to build complementary expertise. A minor is preferred; a maximum of 6 hours can double-count between major and minor.
