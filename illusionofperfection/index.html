The Illusion of Perfection

Scenario: You are applying for a highly competitive university engineering program. You struggled to write the personal statement, so you pasted a few bullet points about your life into an AI tool and asked it to "write a professional college essay."

The AI instantly produced a draft with flawless grammar and impressive vocabulary. It sounds incredibly professional. But there is a massive problem: It doesn't sound like a human.

Large Language Models function by predicting the most statistically probable sequence of words. When asked to write a personal narrative, they default to a homogenized, cliché-ridden "academic" tone. This phenomenon actively erases your unique voice and lived experience.

> ADMISSIONS REALITY: Admissions officers read thousands of essays. They instantly recognize "AI-Speak" (words like delve, tapestry, testament). An AI essay signals a lack of authenticity and diminishes your human agency.

Your Task: You must audit the AI's output, strip away the statistically average clichés, and rewrite the essay using your actual, grounded human experiences.

The Core Lesson

In the paper "Comprehensive AI Literacy: The Case for Centering Human Agency", the authors stress that using AI is not a neutral act. Generative AI fundamentally flattens language, removing the quirks, specifics, and vulnerabilities that make human writing compelling.

When writing about your own life, outsourcing the prose to an LLM constitutes a surrender of agency. By forcing the AI out and putting specific details (like a bent paperclip on a Sunday morning) back in, you proved that the most valuable part of writing is the lived, human experience—something a machine can never replicate.

Reflection Assignment

  • Why does "perfect" AI grammar often result in a weaker, less persuasive personal essay?
  • In what writing scenarios is it ethical/useful to use AI, and in which scenarios is it actively harmful to your agency?
